Exotic dances moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, My favourite dances are as follows, with definitions.

Polynesian chicken
dance A little know but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a small village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".

Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fired as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved hudding close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffing their bodies around in amperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modern styles such as Twerking.

Arctic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterized br robotic, stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
